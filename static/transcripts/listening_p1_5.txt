FCE Listening — Part 1  (task #5)


--- Extract 1 ---
Extract one. You hear a woman leaving a voicemail about a birthday dinner.
[female1] Hi, it’s Lara. Just a quick message about Dad’s birthday dinner on Saturday: we’ve moved it to 7.30 because the restaurant couldn’t fit us in earlier. Don’t bring a big present—he asked for just a card. Call me back if you can’t make it.

Q: What does Lara want the listener to do?
  A) Arrive later than originally planned ✓
  B) Buy a more suitable gift
  C) Change the restaurant booking name

--- Extract 2 ---
Extract two. You hear two friends talking about a music festival.
[male1] So, are you still up for the festival on Sunday? I heard the main band cancelled, which is a bit of a shame.
[female1] Yeah, but I’m going anyway. I’m more excited about the smaller stages, and the tickets weren’t exactly cheap, so I’m not wasting mine.

Q: Why does the woman still plan to go?
  A) She expects the headline act to return
  B) She wants to get value from her ticket ✓
  C) She has promised to meet the band

--- Extract 3 ---
Extract three. You hear a man making an announcement at a wedding reception.
[male1] Ladies and gentlemen, if I could have your attention. The photographer would like to take the group photo in the garden in about ten minutes, before it gets too dark. Please keep your drinks with you, and try not to wander off to the bar just yet.

Q: What is the speaker asking guests to do?
  A) Go outside soon for a picture ✓
  B) Return their glasses to the tables
  C) Move indoors because of the weather

Extract four. You hear a radio presenter talking about a local parade.
[male1] If you’re heading into town for the parade this afternoon, leave the car at home. Several streets will be closed from midday, and buses are being diverted. The good news is, the organisers have added extra viewing areas along the river, so it shouldn’t feel quite so crowded.

Q: What advice does the presenter give?
  A) Use public transport rather than driving ✓
  B) Arrive early to find a seat
  C) Watch the parade from the main square

--- Extract 5 ---
Extract five. You hear two colleagues discussing an office celebration.
[female1] Are we really doing the retirement party in the meeting room? It feels a bit dull for someone who’s been here thirty years.
[male1] I know, but the café downstairs was fully booked. I’m going to bring some photos from old projects and put them up, so at least it’ll feel more personal.

Q: How does the man plan to improve the event?
  A) By changing the location at the last minute
  B) By adding something meaningful to the room ✓
  C) By inviting more people from other departments

--- Extract 6 ---
Extract six. You hear a woman talking to a neighbour about a street party.
[female1] Just to let you know, the street party starts at two, but the noisy bit—music and games—finishes by seven. We’ve told everyone to keep the pavement clear for prams and wheelchairs. If it’s too much, come round and we’ll save you some food to take home.

Q: What is the woman trying to do?
  A) Reassure her neighbour about possible disruption ✓
  B) Persuade her neighbour to organise activities
  C) Warn her neighbour to park elsewhere

--- Extract 7 ---
Extract seven. You hear a man speaking about a charity run at a school assembly.
[male1] Thanks to everyone who took part in Friday’s charity run. We didn’t break the school record, but we raised enough to buy new sports equipment for the younger classes. Next time, remember to hand in your sponsor forms by Monday so we can count every donation.

Q: What is the main point of the talk?
  A) To congratulate students and explain the result ✓
  B) To complain about low participation
  C) To announce a change to the sports timetable

--- Extract 8 ---
Extract eight. You hear two friends discussing plans for New Year’s Eve.
[male2] I’m thinking of going into the city for fireworks, but last year it took us an hour just to get home. I can’t face that again.
[female2] Then let’s stay near mine. We can watch the display on TV, cook something nice, and actually hear ourselves talk. It’s not as exciting, but it’s a lot less stressful.

Q: What do they decide to do?
  A) Go out early to avoid the crowds
  B) Celebrate at home to keep things simple ✓
  C) Travel to a smaller town for the fireworks
