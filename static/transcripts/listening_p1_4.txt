FCE Listening — Part 1  (task #4)


--- Extract 1 ---
Extract one. You hear a woman leaving a voicemail for her landlord.
[female1] Hi, it’s Sofia in flat 3B. The shower keeps switching from hot to freezing, and this morning the water stopped completely for five minutes. I’ve tried the usual things, but it’s getting worse—could you send someone round this week?

Q: Why is the woman contacting the landlord?
  A) She needs urgent help with an unreliable water supply ✓
  B) She wants permission to replace the bathroom
  C) She is complaining about noisy neighbours

--- Extract 2 ---
Extract two. You hear two friends talking about sharing a flat.
[male1] I’m fine with the small bedroom, honestly, but I don’t want arguments about bills every month.
[female1] Same. Let’s set up one account for rent and utilities, and we both pay in on payday. Then we can stop keeping score over every little thing.

Q: What are they mainly trying to avoid?
  A) Running out of space for their belongings
  B) Disagreements about money ✓
  C) Having to move again soon

--- Extract 3 ---
Extract three. You hear a man speaking in a radio interview about home working.
[male1] When I started working from my kitchen table, I thought it would be relaxing. But I couldn’t switch off, and my back was killing me. Setting up a proper corner with a chair and a routine made home feel like home again.

Q: What is the man’s opinion about working from home?
  A) It is only effective if you create clear boundaries ✓
  B) It is always less stressful than working in an office
  C) It is impossible without a separate room

--- Extract 4 ---
Extract four. You hear a recorded message from a building manager.
[narrator] You will hear an announcement.
[female1] Good evening, residents. Due to essential safety checks, the lifts will be out of service tomorrow from 10 a.m. until 2 p.m. Please use the stairs during this time, and avoid moving large furniture until the work is finished.

Q: What should residents do tomorrow?
  A) Expect the lifts to be unavailable for several hours ✓
  B) Collect new keys from the building office
  C) Move their furniture early in the morning

--- Extract 5 ---
Extract five. You hear a couple viewing a house with an estate agent.
[female1] The living room’s lovely, but the garden backs onto the main road, doesn’t it?
[male2] It does, though the double glazing cuts most of the noise. If quiet outdoor space is your priority, you might prefer the property on Oak Street—it’s smaller inside but much more private.

Q: What is the estate agent suggesting?
  A) They should choose a different house if they want a peaceful garden ✓
  B) The road noise will not be noticeable at all
  C) The Oak Street house is bigger and better value

--- Extract 6 ---
Extract six. You hear a man talking to a neighbour in the hallway.
[male1] Sorry about last night—my cousins turned up unexpectedly and we lost track of time. I know the walls are thin here. Next time I’ll keep it down, and if it ever bothers you, just knock on the door.

Q: What is the man doing?
  A) Making an excuse to avoid changing his behaviour
  B) Apologising and offering a solution for future problems ✓
  C) Complaining about the building’s poor design

--- Extract 7 ---
Extract seven. You hear a woman calling a furniture shop.
[female1] Hi, I’m checking on order 6149—the sofa was meant to arrive today between one and three. I’ve stayed in all afternoon, and nobody’s contacted me. Can you tell me if it’s still coming, or do I need to rebook?

Q: What does the woman want to find out?
  A) Whether her delivery will happen today or needs rearranging ✓
  B) How to assemble the sofa when it arrives
  C) If she can change the colour of her order

--- Extract 8 ---
Extract eight. You hear two people discussing a new place to live.
[male1] The rent’s higher than I planned, but it includes heating, and the train station’s five minutes away.
[female2] True, and it’s furnished, so you won’t need to buy basics. If you add up the travel savings and the bills you won’t pay, it might work out cheaper than it looks.

Q: Why does the woman think the flat could be good value?
  A) It is in a fashionable area with lots to do
  B) The total costs may be lower because some expenses are reduced ✓
  C) The landlord is likely to decrease the rent later
